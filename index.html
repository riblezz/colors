Colors in English

Red

Red is the color of heat and blood, so it is associated with energy, war, danger, strength, and power, as well as passion, desire and love. Red is an emotionally intense color. It is very easy to notice, which is why stop signs, stoplights, and fire equipment are usually painted red.

Orange

Orange creates feelings of freshness, excitement and warmth. It's connected to nature and changing seasons. Because orange has high energy and increases competitiveness, many sports teams choose orange for their branding. Orange also warns of danger and is used on safety equipment.

Yellow

Yellow color is instantly associated with warmth. Combine yellow with other warm colors like red and orange and it can give off excitement and heat. Yellow can also stir mental sharpness because of its kinetic energy. The color yellow is also seen as youthful and eager.

Green

Green evokes feelings like revival, freshness, vitality and peace due to its connection to nature. Green aids in relaxation, as environments with green elements are peaceful and nurturing. Some studies suggest that green improves concentration as well.

Blue

The color blue represents both the sky and the sea and is associated with open spaces, freedom, intuition, imagination, inspiration, and sensitivity. Blue also represents meanings of depth, trust, loyalty, sincerity, wisdom, confidence, stability, faith, and intelligence.

Dark blue

On the outer bands of the color wheel, dark blue is often used to represent elegance, authority, and intelligence. Its hex code is #00008B. Dark blue can be seen as serious and is, in fact, the color of mourning in Korea. However, as a shade of a primary color, it combines well for use in most projects.

Purple

As a once-royal color, purple symbolizes wisdom, power, spirituality, luxury, wealth and nobility. Since it is between red and blue, purple is known for the combination of red's power and blue's stability. The color purple also symbolizes independence and dignity.

Pink

As the mix between red's passion and white's purity, pink symbolizes love, nurture and compassion. It evokes feelings of comfort, warmth and hope. Pink is also a sign of good health with the phrase “in the pink.” It symbolizes success in the expression that “everything is rosy” and happiness with “tickled pink.”

Brown

The color brown represents natural landscapes, dependability, strength, and warmth.

Gray

Gray is the color most commonly associated in many cultures with the elderly and old age, because of the association with grey hair; it symbolizes the wisdom and dignity that come with experience and age.

Black

It is an achromatic color, without hue, like white and grey. It is often used symbolically or figuratively to represent darkness. Black and white have often been used to describe opposites such as good and evil, the Dark Ages versus Age of Enlightenment, and night versus day.

White

White is often associated with purity, perfection, honesty, cleanliness, and beginnings. Surveys in Europe and the United States repeatedly link the color white to forms of purity.
